# Ann’s Garden Planner — Project Brief

## Purpose

Ann’s Garden Planner is a mobile-first web app for Ann’s one-acre garden in
North Somerset, England.

The app should help Ann, Mark and Alicia know what garden tasks need doing,
avoid missing time-sensitive seasonal jobs, track plants and areas, record
notes/photos, and improve the garden year by year.

The app is primarily for Ann’s personal use, not a public product from day one.

## Primary users

- Ann — main user and garden owner
- Alicia — gardener, around 3 hours per week
- Mark — occasional helper/contributor

All users work on the same shared garden.

## Main problem

Ann currently keeps garden notes in different places. This means useful ideas,
seasonal tasks and improvement plans can be forgotten until it is too late.

The app should create one reliable place for:

- monthly garden tasks
- plant records
- garden areas
- notes
- photos
- task history
- future plans
- reminders inside the app

## Core product promise

When Ann opens the app, she should quickly know:

> What needs doing now in my actual garden?

The app should feel relevant to Ann’s garden, not generic.

## Garden context

Location:

- North Somerset, near Bristol, England
- One-acre rural garden
- Walled on three sides
- Rare frost
- Mostly clay soil
- Mostly neutral soil
- Some dry areas under large trees
- South-facing sunny areas
- Shady borders under trees
- No irrigation system
- UK gardening calendar assumed

## Initial garden areas

- Ericaceous border, south
- Shady borders under trees
- Main lawn area
- South-facing borders
- Orchard area, lower field
- Veg beds, lower field
- Driveway borders
- Patio
- Containers

## Product feel

The app should feel:

- calm
- practical
- friendly
- visual where useful
- quick to use
- mobile-first
- garden-specific
- not bossy
- not over-educational

Ann said the app should feel like:

> my friend

It should not feel like:

> a bossy teacher

## Success criteria

The app is successful if Ann can:

- know what tasks need doing now
- avoid missing pruning and seasonal jobs
- coordinate clearly with Alicia
- track plants and garden areas
- keep notes in one place
- remember what worked and what did not
- compare garden photos over time
- review what happened last year
- avoid irrelevant prompts
