# User Flows

## Flow 1 — Ann checks what needs doing

1. Ann opens the app on her iPhone.
2. The first screen is Tasks.
3. She sees:
   - due now
   - overdue
   - upcoming
   - progress summary
4. She taps a task.
5. She reads short practical guidance.
6. She marks the task:
   - done
   - partially done
   - postponed
   - skipped
   - not applicable
7. She optionally adds a note, photo or time spent.
8. The task disappears from the active list if completed.
9. The task remains in history.

## Flow 2 — Alicia completes a task

1. Alicia opens the app.
2. She uses her profile.
3. She sees current tasks.
4. She marks work completed.
5. The completion records Alicia as the person who did it.
6. Ann can review Alicia’s completed work later.

## Flow 3 — Ann adds a plant

1. Ann opens Garden.
2. She chooses Plants.
3. She taps Add plant.
4. She enters known information.
5. If she does not know the name, she can mark it as unknown.
6. She optionally links the plant to an area.
7. She adds a photo if useful.
8. The plant appears in the plant list and area profile.

## Flow 4 — Ann adds a garden area

1. Ann opens Garden.
2. She chooses Areas.
3. She taps Add area.
4. She enters name and notes.
5. She optionally adds sunlight, soil and drainage notes.
6. The area becomes available for plants, photos, notes and tasks.

## Flow 5 — Ann creates a quick diary note

1. Ann opens Diary.
2. She enters a quick note.
3. She optionally links it to:
   - area
   - plant
   - task
4. She optionally adds a photo.
5. She saves the note.
6. The note becomes searchable and visible in related history.

## Flow 6 — Ann creates a more detailed reflection

1. Ann starts a diary entry.
2. She expands the optional detail fields.
3. She records:
   - what went well
   - what went badly
   - what to try next
4. She optionally creates a follow-up task.

## Flow 7 — Ann uploads a photo

1. Ann taps Add photo.
2. She selects or takes a photo.
3. She links it to an area, plant, task or diary note.
4. She adds an optional caption.
5. The photo appears in the relevant timeline.

## Flow 8 — Ann compares garden photos

1. Ann opens Photos or an Area.
2. She selects two photos.
3. The app shows them side by side.
4. Ann can add a note about what changed.

## Flow 9 — Ann plans bulb planting

1. Ann creates a planned task.
2. She records bulb type, quantity and area.
3. She adds a purchase note if needed.
4. The task appears in the relevant month.
5. Later, Ann records results and photos.

## Flow 10 — Ann reviews last year

1. Ann opens a task, plant, area or month.
2. The app shows previous notes and completions.
3. Ann uses this to decide what to do differently this year.
